What is AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation)?

What is AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation)?

Answer Engine Optimisation is the practice of making your business the source that AI assistants like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Alexa quote when people ask about your services. AEO is SEO for the AI era.

AEO works by making your website content clear, structured, factual, and easy for answer engines to understand. It uses direct explanations, helpful FAQs, schema markup, and service pages that answer real customer questions.

Cloud Hak uses AEO alongside SEO and Web Design & Development. The goal is to make your business easier to cite in AI answers and easier to trust when a customer compares options.

FAQ

Common questions about AEO (Answer Engine Optimisation)

What is AEO?

Answer Engine Optimisation is the practice of structuring your website content so AI assistants like ChatGPT and Google AI Overviews quote your business when answering relevant questions. It focuses on clear answers, factual service information, and schema markup.

Is AEO replacing SEO?

No, AEO complements SEO. SEO targets search engines, while AEO targets AI answer engines, and most businesses need both as customer search behaviour changes.

How do you measure AEO success?

We track AI citations, featured snippet appearances, AI Overview visibility where available, and organic traffic from answer-led searches. We also review whether your service pages answer the questions customers actually ask.

How quickly does AEO work?

AEO usually takes 2-4 months for initial AI citations or answer visibility signals. The timeline depends on your existing authority, website structure, content quality, and competition.

How much does AEO cost?

AEO starts from £200/month as an add-on to SEO or £400/month as a standalone service. The cost depends on the number of services, FAQs, schema types, and reporting requirements.

What is structured data?

Structured data is code on your website that helps AI and search engines understand your content. Common examples include FAQ schema, LocalBusiness schema, and Service schema.
