Patient Triage Framework

How to route every lead efficiently — Jessica handles intake, Dr. Sia handles treatment
⚡ The Problem

Every lead currently goes straight to Dr. Sia. With 30-40 leads/month growing to 60+, he becomes the bottleneck. His time should be spent treating patients, not doing initial data collection that someone else can handle.

The fix: a simple triage system where Jessica collects all the information first, categorises the case, and Dr. Sia only gets involved when clinical expertise is actually needed.

🔄 The Flow
1. Lead arrives (website / phone / email)
Nima's CRM — logged & acknowledged within 1 hour
↓

Jessica — follows the framework below

🟢 GREEN — Jessica Handles Fully

Jessica does the full intake, books the appointment, and manages the patient. Dr. Sia is not involved until treatment day.

Lead TypeExamplesWhat Jessica Does
General enquiries Pricing questions, opening hours, location, what treatments are offered Answer questions, provide info, book consultation if interested
Simple bookings Returning patients booking follow-ups, routine check-ups Schedule directly, confirm appointment
Referrals from other dentists Dentist refers patient with a clear treatment need already identified Collect referral letter, book consultation with Dr. Sia, prepare file
Post-treatment follow-ups Existing patients checking in, reporting minor issues, appliance adjustments Triage the issue, book adjustment if needed, escalate to Dr. Sia if serious

🔵 BLUE — Jessica Preps, Dr. Sia Treats

Jessica does the full intake, gathers all clinical information, prepares a summary, and books the patient with Dr. Sia. Dr. Sia reviews the summary before the appointment so no time is wasted.

Lead TypeExamplesWhat Jessica Gathers Before Dr. Sia
TMJ / jaw pain Grinding, clenching, jaw tension, clicking Pain duration, severity (1-10), triggers (morning/night), previous treatments, stress levels
Sleep apnea / snoring Adult breathing issues during sleep, fatigue Sleep study results if any, partner observations, BMI, fatigue level, previous diagnosis
Children — growth & airway Mouth breathing, crowding, dummy use, bite issues Child's age, specific symptoms, duration, ENT history (tonsils/adenoids), dummy/thumb habits
Orthodontic relapse Previous braces, teeth moved back When braces were worn, what was treated, current concerns, retainers used
Cosmetic / aesthetic Teeth appearance, gaps, discolouration Specific concern photos, timeline expectations, budget
Adult airway / breathing Narrow jaw, nasal breathing difficulty Breathing symptoms, sleep quality, previous ortho/extractions, ENT history

Key: Dr. Sia receives a 1-page summary before every BLUE appointment — symptoms, history, and Jessica's intake notes. He walks in already knowing the case.

🔴 RED — Dr. Sia Same-Day

These need Dr. Sia's attention immediately. Jessica contacts him directly (not next available appointment).

Red FlagWhyAction
Severe acute pain Possible infection, fracture, or acute TMJ episode Same-day emergency slot
Child with severe breathing issues / possible sleep apnea Airway compromise in children is urgent — affects brain development Priority booking within 48 hours
Post-treatment complication Appliance broken, severe pain after procedure, infection signs Same-day, Dr. Sia assesses directly
Dental trauma Accident, injury to teeth or jaw Same-day emergency
📋 Jessica's Intake Script

Jessica asks these questions on the first call. Takes 15-20 minutes. The answers determine the triage colour.

For all patients:

  • Full name, date of birth (or child's DOB if parent calling)
  • Phone number and email
  • How did you hear about us? (referral source tracking)
  • What is the main reason you're contacting us?
  • How long has this been an issue?
  • Have you seen another dentist or doctor about this? What did they say?
  • Are you currently in any pain? (Rate 1-10)
  • Any previous dental treatments, braces, or extractions?
  • Any medical conditions or medications we should know about?
  • Do you have dental insurance?

For children specifically:

  • Does your child breathe through their mouth or nose?
  • Does your child snore at night?
  • Has your child used a dummy / thumb-sucks? Until what age?
  • Has your child had their tonsils or adenoids checked?
  • Any feeding difficulties as a baby? (tongue tie, breastfeeding issues)
  • Has your child had any speech issues?

For TMJ / grinding specifically:

  • When did the pain/tension start?
  • Is it worse in the morning (night grinding) or during the day?
  • Does your partner notice you grinding at night?
  • Any clicking, popping, or locking of the jaw?
  • Have you used a night guard before? Did it help?
  • Are you under significant stress currently?
